Education and Career Planning

College campus and academic advising
Step 1

Confirm the Academic Pathway

  • Confirm the Bachelor of Arts in Political Science degree plan.
  • Meet with an APSU academic advisor to review requirements and sequencing.
  • Confirm the language focus and expected graduation timeline.
  • Ask how study abroad credits may apply to the degree plan.
  • Meet with Career Services to connect coursework with public service, law, government, or international affairs goals.

Study abroad and France experience
Step 2

Plan France Study Abroad

  • Best timing: freshman or sophomore year.
  • Recommended length: a two-week short-term program.
  • Supports language development, cultural exposure, and international experience.
  • Estimated cost range: $4,300-$7,700.
  • Planning estimate: approximately $6,000.

Washington, DC government internship
Step 3

Prepare for Washington, DC

  • Best timing: junior or senior year.
  • Program example: The Washington Center Academic Internship Program.
  • Supports government, public policy, law, and international affairs goals.
  • Summer 2026 fees: $7,500 program, $4,990 housing, and $60 application.
  • Spring/Fall 2026 fees: $9,700 program, $6,570 housing, and $60 application.

Scholarship and financial planning
Step 4

Sequence the Commitments

  • Do not compress both programs into one summer unless funding is already secured.
  • Year 1-2: complete France study abroad planning and continue language coursework.
  • Year 3-4: pursue Washington, DC internship experience closer to graduation.
  • This sequence spreads out cost, deadlines, travel demands, and academic planning.
  • It also creates a stronger career narrative for graduate school or federal employment.
Advising note Study abroad first; Washington, DC internship closer to graduation.
Advising Recommendation

Recommended Pathway

The recommended pathway is to use the first two academic years to strengthen coursework, language exposure, and international experience through a shorter France program, then use the final two years to pursue a Washington, DC internship aligned with Simone's career goals.

  1. First advising appointment Confirm the Political Science degree plan, expected graduation date, language plans, and how experiential credits may apply.
  2. Freshman or sophomore year Plan for a two-week France study abroad program that supports language learning, confidence, and international exposure.
  3. One year before a DC application Build a resume, identify policy interests, meet Career Services, and compare Washington, DC program terms and costs.
  4. Junior or senior year Apply for The Washington Center or a comparable DC-based internship program when the experience can support post-graduation goals.
  5. Before final commitment Confirm scholarships, credit transfer, housing, travel, food, local transportation, and emergency funds in writing.
Cost Planning

Use published fees as a starting point, not the final budget.

Program fees often exclude personal expenses, airfare, passport costs, meals, local transportation, course materials, and emergency funds. Simone should verify all figures with the current program office before committing.

Planning caution: Do not schedule France study abroad and a Washington, DC internship in the same summer unless scholarships, aid, and family contributions are already confirmed.

Scholarships and Support

Funding should be planned before program selection is final.

Funding decisions should be part of the academic plan, not a final step after travel plans are already fixed. Simone should document deadlines, eligibility, award amounts, and whether each award can apply to program fees, housing, travel, or personal expenses.

Gilman Scholarship

The Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholarship may support eligible U.S. undergraduate students with financial need who study or intern abroad.

Next Steps Checklist
