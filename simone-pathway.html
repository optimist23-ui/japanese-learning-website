Education and Career Planning

A structured pathway for France study abroad and a Washington, D.C. internship.

This guide organizes Simone's academic, international, and career development options into a recommended sequence that supports a Political Science degree, language growth, federal career exploration, and responsible financial planning.

Review the Pathway See Next Steps

Planning Snapshot

  • Recommended sequence Study abroad first; Washington, D.C. internship closer to graduation.
  • France planning estimate Approximately $6,000, with listed examples ranging from $4,300-$7,700.
  • D.C. program estimate Summer 2026: $12,550 before personal expenses; Spring/Fall 2026: $16,330 before personal expenses.
  • Funding focus Scholarships, academic advising, early deadlines, and written cost planning.
Recommended Pathway

Four planning areas to coordinate before making commitments.

The original workflow has been organized into advisory cards that can be used in meetings with academic advisors, career services, study abroad staff, and family financial planning discussions.

College campus and academic advising
Step 1

Academic Pathway

  • Confirm the Bachelor of Arts in Political Science degree plan.
  • Meet with an APSU academic advisor to review requirements and sequencing.
  • Confirm the language focus and expected graduation timeline.
  • Ask how study abroad credits may apply to the degree plan.
  • Meet with Career Services for long-term planning toward public service, law, government, or international affairs.
Primary source: APSU Political Science
Study abroad and France experience
Step 2

France Study Abroad

  • Best timing: freshman or sophomore year.
  • Recommended length: a two-week short-term program.
  • Supports language development, cultural exposure, and international experience.
  • Estimated cost range: $4,300-$7,700.
  • Planning estimate: approximately $6,000.
Primary sources: APSU Study Abroad France Program Example
Washington, D.C. public service internship
Step 3

Washington, D.C. Program

  • Best timing: junior or senior year.
  • Program: The Washington Center Academic Internship Program.
  • Supports government, public policy, law, and international affairs goals.
  • Summer 2026 fees: $7,500 program, $4,990 housing, and $60 application.
  • Spring/Fall 2026 fees: $9,700 program, $6,570 housing, and $60 application.
Primary sources: The Washington Center Fees TWC Cost Example
Scholarship and financial planning
Step 4

Recommended Sequence

  • Do not compress both programs into one summer unless funding is already secured.
  • Year 1-2: complete France study abroad planning and continue language coursework.
  • Year 3-4: pursue Washington, D.C. internship experience closer to graduation.
  • This sequence spreads out cost, deadlines, travel demands, and academic planning.
  • It also creates a stronger career narrative for graduate school or federal employment.
Advising note: Study abroad first; D.C. internship closer to graduation.
Advising Recommendation

Recommended Pathway

The recommended pathway is to use the first two academic years to strengthen coursework, language exposure, and international experience through a shorter France program, then use the final two years to pursue a Washington, D.C. internship aligned with career goals.

  1. First advising appointment Confirm the Political Science degree plan, expected graduation date, language plans, and how experiential credits may apply.
  2. Freshman or sophomore year Plan for a two-week France study abroad program that supports language learning, confidence, and international exposure.
  3. One year before D.C. application Build a resume, identify policy interests, meet Career Services, and compare Washington, D.C. program terms and costs.
  4. Junior or senior year Apply for The Washington Center or a comparable D.C.-based internship program when the experience can support post-graduation goals.
  5. Before final commitment Confirm scholarships, credit transfer, housing, travel, food, local transportation, and emergency funds in writing.
Funding and Support

Scholarship planning should begin before program selection is final.

Funding decisions should be treated as part of the academic plan, not as a final step after travel plans are already fixed. Simone should document deadlines, eligibility, award amounts, and whether each award can apply to program fees, housing, travel, or personal expenses.

Gilman Scholarship

The Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholarship may support eligible U.S. undergraduate students with financial need who study or intern abroad.

Review Gilman program overview

Campus Funding Questions

Ask whether institutional scholarships, departmental awards, financial aid, or external grants may apply to short-term study abroad or internship programs.

Cost Review

Compare published program fees with additional expenses such as flights, passports, meals, local transportation, course materials, and emergency funds.

Planning caution: Do not schedule France study abroad and a Washington, D.C. internship in the same summer unless scholarships, aid, and family contributions are already confirmed.
Action Plan

Next Steps Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare for advising conversations and avoid committing to programs before academic credit, cost, and timing are clear.

  • Schedule an APSU academic advising meeting to confirm degree requirements and course sequencing.
  • Ask how study abroad credits would apply to Simone's Political Science degree plan.
  • Meet with the study abroad office to review France program options, deadlines, deposits, and cancellation terms.
  • Create a written cost estimate for France using the $6,000 planning figure and any updated program quote.
  • Meet with Career Services to map Washington, D.C. internship timing to career goals.
  • Review The Washington Center fees for the intended term and add housing, travel, food, and local transportation estimates.
  • Research Gilman and campus scholarship deadlines at least one term before applying.
  • Decide whether the preferred sequence remains Year 1-2 for France and Year 3-4 for Washington, D.C.
